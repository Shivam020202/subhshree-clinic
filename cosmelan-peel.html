Professional Cosmelan Peel Treatment

Experience the gold standard in depigmentation therapy with our professional Cosmelan Peel treatment. This advanced medical-grade peel is specifically designed to treat melasma, hyperpigmentation, and stubborn dark spots that resist other treatments. Our expert dermatologists use this revolutionary system to help you achieve clearer, more even-toned skin with long-lasting results.

The Cosmelan method is a unique depigmentation treatment that works by inhibiting tyrosinase enzyme activity, effectively reducing melanin production at the cellular level. This comprehensive approach ensures not only immediate visible results but also prevents future pigmentation from forming, making it the most effective treatment for melasma and chronic hyperpigmentation conditions.

  • Treats melasma, post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation, and age spots
  • Suitable for all skin types and phototypes
  • Provides long-lasting depigmentation results
  • Minimal downtime with professional aftercare support
  • Prevents future pigmentation formation

What is Cosmelan Peel?

Cosmelan is a professional depigmentation method that consists of two phases: an intensive in-clinic treatment followed by a home maintenance program. The treatment uses a unique combination of active ingredients including kojic acid, phytic acid, and arbutin to effectively inhibit melanin production. This two-phase approach ensures maximum efficacy while maintaining skin safety and comfort throughout the treatment process.

The Cosmelan system is considered the most effective depigmentation treatment available, with clinical studies showing up to 95% improvement in melasma and hyperpigmentation cases. The treatment works by targeting multiple pathways of melanin synthesis, ensuring comprehensive and long-lasting results.

What Causes Hyperpigmentation and Melasma?

Understanding the root causes of pigmentation disorders helps us provide the most effective treatment approach. Various factors contribute to hyperpigmentation and melasma that Cosmelan peel can effectively address:

Hormonal Changes

Pregnancy, oral contraceptives, and hormone replacement therapy can trigger melasma by increasing melanin production in response to hormonal fluctuations.

UV Radiation Exposure

Prolonged sun exposure stimulates melanocytes to produce excess melanin, leading to age spots, sun spots, and worsening of existing pigmentation.

Post-Inflammatory Response

Acne, injuries, or aggressive treatments can cause inflammation that triggers excess melanin production in the healing process.

Genetic Predisposition

Family history and genetic factors determine individual susceptibility to developing pigmentation disorders and melasma.

Additional Contributing Factors:
  • Certain medications that increase photosensitivity
  • Thyroid disorders and autoimmune conditions
  • Stress and lack of adequate sleep
  • Use of harsh skincare products or treatments
  • Age-related changes in melanin distribution
